# OpenCCR Project Licensing

The OpenCCR project operates as a dual-entity ecosystem dedicated to establishing an open, robust standard for rebreather systems. To support both open-source community development and secure commercial integration, the assets in this repository are divided into distinct legal categories based on their technical domain.

By interacting with, modifying, or manufacturing assets within this repository, you agree to abide by the respective licenses detailed below.

---

## 1. Hardware Designs & Documentation

All physical hardware files, including but not limited to schematics, printed circuit board (PCB) layouts, Gerber manufacturing files, Bill of Materials (BOM), CAD models, and mechanical drawings, are licensed under:

### CERN Open Hardware Licence v2 - Strongly Reciprocal (CERN-OHL-S)

* **What this means:** You are free to view, modify, and manufacture these hardware modules. However, if you distribute manufactured hardware or modified design files, you **must** make those modifications and the complete source hardware files publicly available under the exact same CERN-OHL-S terms.
* **Full Text:** The complete legal text for this license can be found in this repository at `/licenses/CERN-OHL-S.txt`.

---

## 2. Software & Firmware Core

All source code, firmware scripts, APIs, communication protocol implementations, and software binaries contained within this repository are licensed under:

### GNU General Public License v3 (GPLv3)

* **What this means:** You can run, study, share, and modify the software core. However, if you convey or distribute modified versions of this software (or devices running it), you **must** release your entire modified source code ecosystem under the GPLv3, ensuring it remains open forever.
* **Full Text:** The complete legal text for this license can be found in this repository at `/licenses/GPLv3.txt`.

---

## 3. Commercial & Closed-Source Licensing Exceptions

The strongly reciprocal nature of CERN-OHL-S and GPLv3 means that commercial manufacturers who wish to integrate OpenCCR components into proprietary, closed-source, or non-disclosed rebreather products **cannot** do so under the public terms above.

To accommodate commercial production and custom hardware consolidation onto single, proprietary PCBs, alternative proprietary licenses are available.

Commercial licensing exemptions, engineering integration, and safety-critical compliance consulting are handled exclusively through our commercial entity.

* **Inquiries:** Please contact licensing@openccr.io for enterprise licensing terms.
