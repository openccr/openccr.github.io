Platform Vision

A Fully Functional Electronic CCR

openCCR is designed from the ground up as a complete electronic closed circuit rebreather platform. The hardware spans three interconnected domains: custom electronics boards for oxygen sensing, loop control, depth measurement, and diver alerting; mechanical designs for the rebreather body, pressure housings, and breathing loop interfaces; and embedded firmware and a companion app to tie it all together into a coherent research platform. Every domain is fully open, documented, and designed to evolve independently as the community builds and refines it.

Electronic Design Files

Complete KiCad project for all openCCR boards. Schematics, BOM, Gerbers, and fabrication data in one repository. Licensed under CERN-OHL-S.

Schematics

Multi-sheet KiCad schematics covering all subsystems. Each sheet includes design-rationale annotations, net labels, and power-domain boundaries. Revision blocks track hardware versions across releases.

Bill of Materials

Fully annotated BOM with component values, tolerances, package types, and supplier part numbers for Digi-Key and Mouser. Includes BOM variants for different PO₂ cell connector types and regional availability.

Gerber & Fabrication Data

Gerber files, drill maps, and pick-and-place centroid data for each board. Design rules checked against JLCPCB and PCBWay specifications for cost-effective prototyping and small-run production.

PCB Designs

Platform Boards

Each board is a standalone module connected via CCRAN — the openCCR community's effort to define a standard CAN-bus-based communication layer for rebreather electronics. Unlike proprietary bus protocols, CCRAN is designed for interoperability: any vendor can build a board that speaks CCRAN and it will integrate with the rest of the system. Boards can be updated or replaced independently as the design evolves.

Controller Board

Main system brain. Built around the proven, low-energy nRF52 or nRF54 microcontroller family. Hosts power management circuitry, CCRAN bus master logic, USB and BLE interfaces for companion app connectivity, and on-board flash for dive log storage. Orchestrates all other boards, runs the primary firmware stack, and can also execute onboard decompression calculations.

Sensor Conditioning Boards

Modular front-end boards available in multiple variants to accommodate different cell types and configurations. Variants support analog (galvanic) oxygen cells, digital solid-state oxygen cells, and CO₂ cells, in various combinations and channel counts. Each board outputs calibrated readings and cell health status over CCRAN.

Pressure Sensor Board

Measures ambient water pressure to determine current diving depth. Depth data feeds into the control loop for automatic setpoint changeover, decompression calculations, and dive log recording. Communicates sensor readings to the controller board over CCRAN.

Solenoid Driver Boards

Driver boards for oxygen addition valves. Multiple variants support different valve types, including standard on/off solenoids and proportional valves for precise gas flow control. Includes current-sense feedback for valve health monitoring and fly-back protection. Receives actuation commands over CCRAN.

HUD (Head-Up Display) Board

Diver-facing optical indicator module. Drives a tri-color LED array for PO₂ status, setpoint indication, and alarm signalling. Designed to be mounted in front of the diver's mask. Receives display commands over CCRAN.

Display Boards

Support various display technologies including OLED, TFT, and e-Paper panels. Can serve as a detailed PO₂ monitor showing per-cell readings and trend data, or as a display for the onboard decompression dive computer. Receives data over CCRAN and renders dive-critical information in configurable layouts.

Alert Boards

Implement haptic or visual feedback for critical events, designed to alert both the diver and their dive buddy when dangerous conditions arise. Multiple form factors in development for wrist-mounted, loop-mounted, and buddy-alert configurations. Receives alarm events over CCRAN.

Many More…

The CCRAN bus architecture is designed to be extensible. Future and community-developed boards can join the bus by implementing the CCRAN node specification — enabling new sensor types, display formats, alerting mechanisms, and integrations without modifying the core system.

Repository — openccr/mechanics

Mechanical Design Files

STEP, STL, and 2D drawing files for all physical components of the openCCR platform. Designed for FDM/SLS 3D printing and CNC machining. Licensed under CERN-OHL-S.

3D CAD Models (STEP & STL)

Parametric STEP files for electronics pressure housing, sensor mounting pods, scrubber canister interfaces, and breathing loop connectors. STL exports for direct 3D printing.

2D Technical Drawings

DXF and PDF drawings with full GD&T annotations, O-ring groove specifications to AS568 standard, and machining tolerances. Each drawing includes a revision block and material callout (6061-T6 aluminium, Delrin, or PETG depending on application).

Pressure & Assembly Notes

Each enclosure design includes assembly notes with torque specifications, O-ring lubrication requirements, and an enclosure pressure-test procedure.
